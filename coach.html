Activity pathways

Each card describes the classroom effect, when to use it, and the best library search to open next.

01
Thinking & Discussion

Help students process, speak, and respond.

Participation

Get More Students Talking

Use when the same few students answer or when students need protected thinking time before speaking.

Strong matches: Think-Pair-Share, Round Robin, Speed Networking Open discussion activities
Rigor

Build Deeper Discussion

Use when student comments remain surface-level and the class needs evidence, listening, and follow-up questions.

Strong matches: Fishbowl, Silent Discussion, Case Study Teams Open deeper discussion activities
Focus

Protect Independent Thinking

Use before collaboration so every student develops an idea, calculation, response, or recommendation of their own.

Strong matches: 3-2-1 Reflection, Exit Ticket, Decision Matrix Open independent activities
02
Application & Decisions

Make business content feel real.

Evidence

Analyze a Case or Business Problem

Use when students must sort evidence, identify stakeholders, weigh tradeoffs, and recommend action.

Strong matches: Case Study Teams, Case Study Carousel, Consulting Firm Open analysis activities
Judgment

Make and Defend a Decision

Use when students need to commit to a position, explain criteria, and respond to competing viewpoints.

Strong matches: Four Corners, Decision Matrix, Board Meeting Open decision activities
Real-World Practice

Run a Business Simulation

Use when students should take on professional roles, respond to changing information, and produce a realistic outcome.

Strong matches: Business Simulation, Product Launch, Consulting Firm Open simulation activities
03
Communication

Strengthen speaking, presenting, and persuasion.

Professional Speaking

Present Clearly and Concisely

Use when students need to organize an idea for an audience without relying on a long traditional slideshow.

Strong matches: TED-Style Talk, Trade Show, Press Conference Open presentation activities
Persuasion

Pitch and Defend an Idea

Use when students must explain value, answer questions, and persuade an audience to support a recommendation.

Strong matches: Shark Tank, Investor Pitch, Product Launch Open pitch activities
Fluency

Build Speaking Confidence

Use repeated, low-stakes speaking rounds when students need practice before a graded presentation or interview.

Strong matches: Speed Networking, Think-Pair-Share, Round Robin Open speaking-practice activities
04
Classroom Needs

Match the activity to the room you have today.

Energy

Add Purposeful Movement

Use when the room feels flat or a long block needs a physical reset without sacrificing academic thinking.

Strong matches: Gallery Walk, Station Rotation, Four Corners Open movement activities
Collaboration

Improve Small-Group Work

Use structures with roles, turn-taking, or shared products when groups tend to stall or divide work unevenly.

Strong matches: Jigsaw, Round Robin, Case Study Teams Open group activities
Access

Differentiate Without Creating Separate Lessons

Use stations, roles, choice, and varied evidence so students can enter the same learning goal in different ways.

Strong matches: Station Rotation, Jigsaw, Gallery Walk Open differentiation activities
20 Minutes or Less

Use a Fast, Low-Prep Structure

Use when you need a meaningful opener, midpoint reset, quick practice cycle, or short closure.

Strong matches: Think-Pair-Share, Round Robin, Exit Ticket Open quick activities
05
Assessment & Closure

See what students know and what should happen next.

Formative Assessment

Check Understanding During the Lesson

Use when you need visible evidence before moving on, reteaching, or changing the level of support.

Strong matches: Whiteboard Relay, Retrieval Practice, Quiz Tournament Open assessment activities
Retrieval

Review Without Another Worksheet

Use active recall, team competition, and error correction to make review more diagnostic and engaging.

Strong matches: Retrieval Practice, Quiz Tournament, Escape Room Open review activities
Reflection

Close the Lesson with Evidence

Use when students need to summarize learning, name confusion, transfer an idea, or identify a next step.

Strong matches: Exit Ticket, 3-2-1 Reflection Open closure activities
