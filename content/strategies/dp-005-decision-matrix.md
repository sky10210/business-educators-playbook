# Decision Matrix

## Classroom Trailer

Four options look promising, but the class cannot choose all of them. Students must decide what matters most before they score anything. Their matrix may identify a winner, but the real learning comes when they explain the weights, challenge the evidence, and test how easily the result could change.

## Summary

Students define criteria, assign importance, score alternatives with evidence, and test whether a recommendation remains strong when assumptions change.

## Example Prompt

A student-run business must choose the best product to launch from four ideas with different costs, demand, risks, and operational requirements.
