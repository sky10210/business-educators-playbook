# Case Study Carousel

## Quick Scan

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Time | 35–75 minutes |
| Prep | Moderate |
| Movement | High |
| Collaboration | High |
| Technology | Optional |

## Best Uses

- AP Business case studies
- Marketing campaigns
- Financial decision making
- Entrepreneurship analysis
- Current events

## Why I Use It

Students analyze several authentic business cases in a single class period. Each group builds on the thinking of previous groups, producing richer discussion and stronger evidence-based recommendations.

## Before Class

Prepare 4–8 case stations with a different focus question at each station. Use a common recording sheet so students compare ideas across every case.

## Materials

- Printed or digital cases
- Recording sheet
- Timer
- Chart paper or shared document

## Room Setup

Arrange numbered stations around the room with enough space for teams to collaborate comfortably.

# Running the Strategy

1. Introduce the overall business challenge.
2. Assign groups to starting stations.
3. Analyze the case and record recommendations.
4. Rotate on the timer.
5. Read the previous group's work before adding new evidence.
6. Complete all stations.
7. Debrief by comparing the strongest recommendations.

## Teacher Language

- Build on the previous team's thinking.
- Cite evidence before making recommendations.
- Disagree professionally.
- Focus on tradeoffs, not just the final answer.

## Timing

### 45 Minutes
- Introduction: 5 min
- Five stations: 6 min each
- Debrief: 10 min

### 75 Minutes
- Introduction: 5 min
- Six stations: 8–9 min each
- Gallery review: 10 min
- Reflection: 10 min

## Course Examples

### Marketing
Compare rebranding campaigns.

### Personal Finance
Evaluate borrowing, investing, and budgeting scenarios.

### Entrepreneurship
Analyze startup funding decisions.

### Economics
Compare policy responses to economic events.

### Sports & Entertainment Marketing
Evaluate sponsorship and promotional strategies.

### AP Business
Rotate through College Board-style business cases and defend strategic recommendations.

## Common Problems

- Groups repeat ideas → Require one new insight at every station.
- Students rush → Limit the number of required responses.
- Uneven participation → Assign rotating roles.

## Variations

- Digital carousel
- Data carousel
- Silent carousel
- Expert carousel

## Assessment

Evaluate:
- Quality of analysis
- Evidence
- Collaboration
- Final recommendation
- Reflection

## Works Best With

Gallery Walk, Station Rotation, Fishbowl, Exit Ticket.

## Final Thoughts

Case Study Carousel exposes students to multiple authentic business situations while encouraging collaborative reasoning and evidence-based decision making.
