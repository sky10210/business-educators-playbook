# Fishbowl

## Quick Scan

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Time | 20–45 minutes |
| Prep | Low |
| Movement | Moderate |
| Discussion | Very High |
| Technology | Optional |

## Best Uses

- Business ethics discussions
- AP Business case analysis
- Leadership and management scenarios
- Marketing debates
- Entrepreneurship decision making

## Why I Use It

Fishbowl allows a small group to model a high-quality business discussion while the rest of the class observes, records evidence, and evaluates the discussion. Students see what effective reasoning sounds like before joining the conversation.

## Before Class

Choose one discussion prompt with multiple defensible viewpoints. Prepare an observation sheet so students outside the circle have an active role.

## Materials

- Inner-circle chairs
- Observation sheet
- Timer
- Discussion prompt

## Room Setup

Arrange an inner discussion circle surrounded by an outer observation circle.

## Teacher Tips

- Give observers a specific job.
- Pause occasionally to summarize key ideas.
- Rotate students so everyone participates.


---

# Running the Strategy

1. Present one business problem with multiple defensible viewpoints.
2. Select 4–8 students for the inner discussion circle.
3. Assign the remaining students as observers with a note sheet.
4. Allow the inner circle to discuss while observers collect evidence.
5. Pause periodically to summarize important ideas.
6. Rotate new students into the discussion.
7. End with a whole-class reflection.

## Teacher Language

- Support your opinion with evidence.
- Build on another person's idea before introducing a new one.
- Ask questions instead of trying to win the debate.
- If the evidence changes your thinking, say so.

## Observer Responsibilities

Observers should track:
- Strong evidence
- Effective questioning
- Business vocabulary
- Tradeoffs discussed
- Recommendations that changed their thinking

## Timing

### 25 Minutes
- Introduction: 3 min
- Fishbowl: 12 min
- Rotation: 5 min
- Debrief: 5 min

### 45 Minutes
- Introduction: 5 min
- Round 1: 15 min
- Rotation: 10 min
- Round 2: 10 min
- Reflection: 5 min

## Business Examples

### Marketing
Should a company respond publicly to a viral complaint?

### Personal Finance
Should a student prioritize debt repayment or investing?

### Entrepreneurship
Which funding source provides the best balance of growth and control?

### Economics
Which government policy best addresses inflation?

### Sports & Entertainment Marketing
Should a team focus on premium pricing or increasing attendance?

### AP Business
Debate competing strategic recommendations from a case and defend the strongest option with evidence.

## Common Problems

- A few students dominate → Use timed speaking opportunities.
- Observers disengage → Give every observer a scoring or evidence task.
- Discussion becomes opinion only → Require evidence before speaking.

## Variations

- Hot Seat Fishbowl
- Silent Fishbowl
- Digital Backchannel
- Socratic Fishbowl

## Assessment

Evaluate:
- Quality of evidence
- Business reasoning
- Collaboration
- Listening
- Reflection

## Works Best With

Think–Pair–Share → Fishbowl → Gallery Walk → Exit Ticket

## Final Thoughts

Fishbowl helps students hear what high-quality business discussions sound like. When observers have meaningful jobs and speakers support every claim with evidence, students become stronger communicators and decision-makers.
