# Speed Networking

## Quick Scan

|Attribute|Value|
|---|---|
|Time|15–35 minutes|
|Prep|Low|
|Movement|High|
|Collaboration|Very High|

## Best Uses

- Icebreakers
- Reviewing key concepts
- Practicing elevator pitches
- Entrepreneurship networking
- Career readiness

## Why I Use It

Students practice communicating with many classmates in a short period of time. Every conversation is focused, timed, and tied to a business objective.

## Before Class

Prepare 5–10 discussion prompts or scenario cards.

Examples:
- Explain your business idea.
- Recommend a marketing strategy.
- Pitch an investment.
- Solve a customer-service problem.

## Materials

- Prompt cards
- Timer
- Reflection sheet

## Room Setup

Two facing lines or two concentric circles.

## Teacher Tips

Keep conversations short, purposeful, and energetic.


---

# Running the Strategy

1. Introduce the networking objective.
2. Pair students and display the first prompt.
3. Give each partner 60–90 seconds to speak.
4. Signal students to rotate to a new partner.
5. Repeat with new prompts.
6. Finish with a written reflection or class debrief.

## Teacher Language

- Introduce yourself professionally.
- Listen first, then respond.
- Build on your partner's ideas.
- Support your recommendation with evidence.

## Timing

### 20 Minutes
- Introduction: 3 min
- Six networking rounds: 2 min each
- Reflection: 5 min

### 40 Minutes
- Introduction: 5 min
- Ten networking rounds: 3 min each
- Debrief: 5 min

## Course Examples

### Marketing
Deliver a 60-second product pitch.

### Personal Finance
Explain a budgeting strategy or investment recommendation.

### Entrepreneurship
Pitch a startup idea to multiple potential investors.

### Economics
Defend a policy recommendation using economic evidence.

### Sports & Entertainment Marketing
Recommend a sponsorship or promotional campaign.

### AP Business
Present and defend strategic recommendations from a case study.

## Common Problems

- Conversations stall → Provide follow-up questions.
- One student dominates → Use equal speaking time.
- Off-topic discussions → Display the prompt throughout the round.

## Variations

- Career Fair Networking
- Investor Pitch Rotation
- QR-Code Prompt Stations
- Silent Networking with written responses

## Assessment

Evaluate:
- Communication
- Business vocabulary
- Evidence
- Professionalism
- Reflection

## Works Best With

Think–Pair–Share, Fishbowl, Exit Ticket, Station Rotation.

## Final Thoughts

Speed Networking builds confidence through repetition. Students practice explaining ideas to many different audiences while developing communication skills that transfer directly to business and career settings.
