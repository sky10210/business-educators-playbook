# Full Business Simulation

## Quick Scan

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Time | 1 class to several weeks |
| Prep | High |
| Collaboration | Very High |
| Technology | Optional |

## Purpose

Students operate as leadership teams making authentic business decisions over multiple rounds. Every decision has measurable consequences that affect future performance.

## Best Uses

- Entrepreneurship
- Marketing
- Personal Finance
- AP Business
- Capstone projects

## Simulation Framework

1. Establish the company and scenario.
2. Assign executive roles.
3. Present the first decision round.
4. Require evidence-based decisions.
5. Reveal results and changing market conditions.
6. Repeat for multiple rounds.
7. End with a board presentation and written reflection.

## Executive Roles

- CEO
- CFO
- COO
- CMO
- HR Director
- Operations Manager
- Data Analyst

## Teacher Language

- Defend every decision with evidence.
- Consider short- and long-term consequences.
- Explain tradeoffs.
- Revise your strategy when conditions change.

## Timing

- Single-period simulation
- Multi-day simulation
- Semester-long simulation

## Assessment

Evaluate:
- Decision quality
- Team collaboration
- Financial reasoning
- Strategic thinking
- Adaptability
- Reflection

## Variations

- Startup Simulation
- Stock Market Simulation
- Restaurant Simulation
- Sports Franchise Simulation
- Manufacturing Simulation
- Retail Simulation
- International Expansion Simulation

## Final Thoughts

Well-designed simulations help students experience business rather than simply read about it. The strongest simulations require continuous analysis, adaptation, communication, and reflection.
