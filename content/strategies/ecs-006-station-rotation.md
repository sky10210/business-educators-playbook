# Station Rotation

## Quick Scan

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Time | 25–75 minutes |
| Prep | High |
| Movement | High |
| Collaboration | High |
| Technology | Optional |

## Best Uses

- Unit review
- Financial literacy practice
- Marketing analysis
- AP Business skill stations
- Entrepreneurship workshops

## Why I Use It

Station Rotation lets students practice multiple business skills during a single lesson. Instead of completing one long activity, students experience several focused tasks that keep energy high while providing frequent feedback.

## Before Class

Design each station around one learning target.

Example sequence:
- Vocabulary review
- Case study
- Data analysis
- Decision challenge
- Reflection

## Materials

- Station directions
- Recording sheet
- Timer
- Supplies unique to each station
- Optional QR codes or digital resources

## Room Setup

Spread stations around the room to reduce congestion and clearly number each location.

## Teacher Tips

- Keep directions visible at every station.
- Test each activity yourself before class.
- Balance difficulty across stations.
- Plan transitions before students arrive.


---

# Running the Strategy

1. Introduce the overall objective and explain each station.
2. Assign groups and starting stations.
3. Start the timer and rotate together.
4. Circulate, coach, and ask probing questions.
5. Finish with a whole-class synthesis and reflection.

## Teacher Language

- Finish the task before rotating.
- Every member should contribute.
- Explain your reasoning, not just your answer.
- Leave the station ready for the next group.

## Timing

### 30 Minutes
- Intro: 5 min
- Four stations: 5 min each
- Debrief: 5 min

### 60 Minutes
- Intro: 5 min
- Six stations: 7–8 min each
- Reflection: 10 min

## Course Examples

### Marketing
Analyze advertising, branding, pricing, and promotion.

### Personal Finance
Budgeting, credit, insurance, and investing stations.

### Entrepreneurship
Business model, marketing, finance, and operations stations.

### Economics
Supply & demand, inflation, GDP, unemployment, and trade.

### Sports & Entertainment Marketing
Sponsorships, promotions, ticket pricing, and fan engagement.

### AP Business
Case analysis, financial interpretation, strategy, and stakeholder decisions.

## Common Problems

- Groups finish early → Include extension questions.
- Bottlenecks → Stagger starting stations.
- Off-task behavior → Use visible timers and defined roles.

## Variations

- Digital QR-code stations
- Teacher-led station
- Challenge station
- Escape-room station
- Choice-board rotation

## Assessment

Assess:
- Completion
- Accuracy
- Collaboration
- Evidence
- Reflection

## Works Best With

Think–Pair–Share, Gallery Walk, Retrieval Practice, Exit Ticket.

## Final Thoughts

Well-designed stations create many short wins during one lesson. Students stay active, receive frequent feedback, and practice multiple business skills in a single class period.
