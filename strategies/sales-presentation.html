Sales Presentation

Students diagnose a customer's needs, present a relevant value proposition, respond to objections, and ask for a realistic next step.

Selling skillsCustomer discoveryPersuasionObjection handling
Teacher quick actionsSee the classroom →How to run it →

Classroom Trailer

Picture the room

The customer does not care about every feature. They care whether the offer solves their problem. Student sales teams must ask questions, listen, adapt the presentation, and earn the right to request a next step.

A Real Classroom Snapshot

Example challenge: Students sell a workplace productivity service to a small business with limited money and concerns about employee adoption.

Students prepare for a real audience with limited attention. The strategy rewards organization, relevance, audience awareness, evidence, and the ability to respond rather than simply reading prepared material.

Why This Strategy Works

Students diagnose a customer's needs, present a relevant value proposition, respond to objections, and ask for a realistic next step.

The audience creates a meaningful reason to revise. Students must choose what matters, organize ideas around a purpose, and communicate so another person can understand or act. Questioning and feedback expose whether the thinking behind the presentation is actually strong.

Best Uses

  • Selling skills
  • Customer discovery
  • Persuasion
  • Objection handling
  • Value propositions
  • Workplace readiness

Teacher Preparation

  1. Define the audience, purpose, time limit, and required next action.
  2. Provide a business challenge with enough evidence for a defensible message.
  3. Set visual, speaking, and question-response expectations.
  4. Prepare a rehearsal checkpoint before graded delivery.
  5. Require an individual reflection or contribution record.

Timing Options

30-Minute Sprint

Brief 5, preparation 10, rehearsal 5, delivery 7, reflection 3.

45-Minute Lesson

Brief 6, planning 14, rehearsal 8, delivery and questions 12, reflection 5.

75-Minute Block

Add evidence analysis, peer coaching, multiple presentation rounds, and revision.

Teacher Language

  • What did the customer say matters most?
  • Which benefit directly addresses that need?
  • Are you answering the objection or repeating the pitch?
  • What evidence reduces the customer's risk?
  • What is a realistic next commitment?

Course Examples

Marketing

Sell a campaign, sponsorship, advertising package, or research service.

Personal Finance

Present an appropriate financial product while explaining cost and risk responsibly.

Entrepreneurship

Sell a startup product to an early customer.

Sports & Entertainment Marketing

Sell season tickets, sponsorship, premium seating, or an event package.

AP Business

Connect customer needs, value proposition, channels, revenue, relationships, and evidence.

Common Problems and Fixes

Students talk before discovering needs

Require customer questions and a needs summary before the pitch.

Presentations list features

Require every feature to connect to a customer benefit and priority.

Objections become arguments

Teach acknowledge, clarify, respond, and confirm.

The close feels aggressive

Use realistic next steps such as a trial, follow-up meeting, sample, or proposal.

Variations

Speed Selling

Use two-minute discovery and two-minute tailored pitches.

Competing Vendors

Several teams sell different solutions to the same customer panel.

Ethical Sales Challenge

Include a product that is not appropriate for every customer and assess responsible judgment.

Account Renewal

Students must retain an existing customer who is considering leaving.

Recorded Sales Call

Students record the interaction and code their questioning, listening, value, and objection handling.

Student Reflection

  • What did the audience need most from your presentation?
  • Which part of the message was clearest?
  • Which question exposed a weakness or missing assumption?
  • What would you cut, add, or reorganize?
  • What professional communication skill will you carry into the next presentation?
