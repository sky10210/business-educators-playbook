Jigsaw

Turn students into specialists who must teach one piece of a larger business problem.

Cooperative LearningExpert GroupsReadingAccountability
Teacher quick actionsJump to Quick Scan →How to run it →

Quick Scan

Attribute Value
Time 25–60 minutes
Prep Moderate
Movement Moderate
Collaboration Very High
Technology Optional

Best Uses

  • Breaking apart complex business topics
  • AP Business case studies
  • Company research
  • Industry analysis
  • Financial literacy units

Why I Use It

Jigsaw prevents one or two students from carrying the learning.

Each student becomes the team's expert on one section of the content. When students return to their home group, everyone depends on one another to understand the complete topic.

That accountability dramatically increases participation and ownership.

Before Class

Divide the lesson into 4–6 balanced sections.

Good topics include:

  • Marketing Mix
  • SWOT Analysis
  • Financial Statements
  • Forms of Business Ownership
  • International Market Entry
  • Stakeholder Groups

Materials

  • Expert packets
  • Home-group organizer
  • Note sheet
  • Timer
  • Optional digital collaboration tool

Room Setup

Seat students in home groups first, then send experts to meet with others studying the same topic.

Teacher Tips

  • Every expert packet should be roughly equal in difficulty.
  • Give students a note-taking organizer before they leave their home group.
  • Hold students accountable for teaching, not just reading.

Running the Strategy

  1. Introduce the overall business problem.
  2. Assign each student one expert topic.
  3. Expert groups meet to master their section.
  4. Students return to their home groups.
  5. Each expert teaches their section.
  6. Home groups complete a synthesis task that requires every expert's information.

Teacher Language

  • Your teammates should understand your topic because of you.
  • Teach the important ideas, not every detail.
  • Ask questions if something is unclear before returning.

Timing

30 Minutes

  • Home group intro: 5 min
  • Expert groups: 10 min
  • Teaching: 10 min
  • Debrief: 5 min

55–60 Minutes

  • Intro: 5 min
  • Expert work: 15 min
  • Home teaching: 20 min
  • Application: 15 min
  • Reflection: 5 min

Business Examples

Marketing

Each expert studies one element of the marketing mix.

Personal Finance

Each expert researches one investment option before teaching the group.

Entrepreneurship

Experts evaluate funding, operations, marketing, and finance.

Economics

Experts analyze different economic indicators.

AP Business

Experts dissect sections of a complex case before the team develops one recommendation.

Common Problems

  • Experts rush → provide note organizers.
  • Students only read → require teaching without reading directly from the page.
  • Uneven participation → grade both expert preparation and home-group contribution.

Variations

  • Digital Jigsaw
  • Video Jigsaw
  • Data Jigsaw
  • Case Study Jigsaw

Assessment

Assess:

  • Expert preparation
  • Teaching quality
  • Accuracy
  • Team synthesis

Works Best With

Think–Pair–Share, Gallery Walk, Consulting Firm, Board Meeting.

Final Thoughts

Jigsaw works because every student becomes essential. When designed well, students stop depending on the teacher and begin depending on one another.
